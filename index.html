Experiences in Limiting Injuries in Boxing

Good Protective Equipment

When entering the ring, each player equips himself with professional protection such as anti-shock nose, mask, tooth protector and body protector. The anti-shock nose helps reduce the force of blows, the mask protects the face from direct blows, while the tooth protector prevents the consequences of strong blows. These devices not only help keep players safe but also play an important role in reducing pressure and risk of injury.

Train Hard

Professional players spend many hours every day practicing both technique and muscle strength. This helps them develop the ability to deal with difficult situations and reduce the risk of injury due to inflexibility. At the same time, strengthening their muscles also helps them be able to resist pressure from strong blows given by their opponents.

Fighter's Skills

In boxing, the ability to dodge and defend is the key to avoiding dangerous blows. Professional players not only focus on attacking but also pay great attention to flexible movements and quick reactions. This helps them avoid many blows and reduces the risk of injury.

Maintain a Healthy Lifestyle

Professional players regularly participate in comprehensive exercise programs to strengthen their entire body. They also pay attention to their diet, ensuring they provide enough nutrients for their bodies and keep their weight stable. In this way, they reduce the risk of injury due to underlying factors and increase the body's resistance.

Be Psychologically Prepared

A strong mentality helps increase concentration and control emotions during competition. This not only helps players avoid dangerous situations but also reduces mental pressure, thereby reducing the risk of injury due to rash decisions and uncontrolled mental states.
